SEO Audits & Strategy

Find out what's holding your organic performance back.

A decade of SEO experience across some of Australia's most competitive verticals — travel aggregators, financial comparison, national tourism, and financial publishing.

SEO isn't a mystery. It's a diagnosis.

Most businesses know they should be "doing SEO" but aren't sure what's actually wrong, or where to start. They've been told they need more content, or better backlinks, or a faster site — but nobody's taken the time to properly diagnose the problem before prescribing a solution.

That's what an audit does. It's a methodical review of your site's technical health, content strategy, competitive positioning, and backlink profile — with a clear, prioritised list of what to fix and why.

My SEO background

I cut my teeth in SEO at Wotif Group, managing organic performance across multiple travel brands competing in one of APAC's most aggressive search landscapes. From there, I led SEO from launch at Comparethemarket.com.au — another brutally competitive vertical — and grew it into a major acquisition channel.

I've also managed SEO strategy at Tourism Queensland, Destination Gold Coast, and The Motley Fool Australia. This isn't theoretical knowledge — it's been tested across high-stakes, high-competition environments.

What's included in an SEO audit

  • Technical SEO review — crawlability, indexation, site speed, Core Web Vitals, structured data, and mobile performance
  • Site architecture analysis — URL structure, internal linking, content hierarchy, and navigation
  • Content audit — existing content performance, thin content identification, keyword gaps, and content cannibalisation
  • Keyword opportunity mapping — where you're ranking, where you should be, and what's realistic to target
  • Competitive analysis — who's outranking you, why, and what you can learn from them
  • Backlink profile review — link quality, toxic links, and opportunities for acquisition
  • Prioritised action plan — what to fix first, what can wait, and expected impact

Who this is for

Businesses that rely on (or want to rely on) organic search as a customer acquisition channel. Whether you've been doing SEO for years and it's plateaued, or you're starting fresh and want to build on solid foundations, an audit gives you a clear map.

What you get

A comprehensive written report with findings, analysis, and a prioritised roadmap. Not a generic template — a document written specifically for your site, by someone who's done this across some of Australia's most competitive search environments. I'll walk you through it on a call so you understand every recommendation.
